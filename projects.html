Selected Projects







  • Online Deterministic Annealing for Unsupervised, Supervised, and Reinforcement Learning

    - A progressively growing competitive-learning neural network architecture with inherent interpretability, robustness, and regularization properties.
    - Based on the principles of vector quantization and annealing optimization.
    - Trained with a gradient-free stochastic approximation algorithm.
    - Progressive Classification and Clustering (arXiv)
    - Memory-efficient Reinforcement Learning (CDC 2021)
    - Adaptive Graph Partitioning (CDC 2021)











  • Real-Time Priority-Based Cooperative Highway Merging for Heterogeneous Autonomous Traffic

    - A novel cooperative highway merging method taking into account heterogeneous, in terms of physical properties and priority, autonomous vehicles.
    - Quadratic programming optimization with safety guarantees operating in real-time.
    - Best student paper award (1st place), ITSC 2021.
    - For more details and extensions visit Nilesh's website.











  • Learning Swarm Interaction Dynamics from Density Evolution

    - Learning swarm interaction laws by observing its density evolution.
    - Modeling the interaction function as a Green's function for appropriately defined linear operator, allows for the hydrodynamic mean-field limit partial integro-differential equations to be efficiently solved as a system of simple PDEs.
    - PDEs numerically solved with FVM (hyperbolic component) and spectral methods (elliptic component).
    - Learning algorithm formulated as iterative PDE-constrained optimization using conjugate gradient method.
    - More details in (CDC 2020)











  • Reconstructing Strange Attractor Dynamics with Spiking Recurrent Neural Networks

    - Recurrent spiking neural network architecture for learning and reconstruction of a strange attractor with partially observable states.
    - Low-dimensional approximation based on Taken's Delay Embedding Theorem.
    - Application in a heart-shaped oscillator and the Lorenz attractor.











  • A Human-Robot Interface based on Surface Electroencephalographic Sensors

    - A Brain-Machine Interface decoding visual attention into montion in 3D space.
    - Suitable for robot teleoperation or as part of a shared-control robotic manipulation scheme.
    - No restrictions imposed on blinking, head movement, etc.
    - Appropriate for upper-extremity amputees.
    - Experiments conducted at the Control Systems Lab, NTUA.
    - More details in (IROS 2020)











  • Human-Robot Collaboration based on Robust Motion Intention Estimation with Prescribed Performance

    - Physical Human-Robot Collaboration (HRC) scheme with adaptive impedance control under unknown robot dynamics - No explicit communication, use of position, velocity and force/torque feedback.
    - Human intention estimation through prescribed performance adaptive estimation law.
    - More details in (ECC 2018)
